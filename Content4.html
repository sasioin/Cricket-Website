  • Birth of cricket
    Cricket originated in England.Cricket was created during Saxon or Norman times by children living in the Weald, an area of dense woodlands and clearings in south-east England that lies across Kent and Sussex.The first definite written reference is from the end of the 16th century.
  • Second most popular sport
    Cricket now enjoyed in over 180 countries around the world, with approximately 2.5 billion fans.It’s most popular in India and Pakistan, though England and Australia have two of the most famous teams.
  • First cricket world cup
    Organized by the International Cricket Council.The event was founded by England, South Africa, and Australia and was first held in 1975. It’s held once every four years, with England taking the cup in 2019, the most recent event held.
  • Wicket bails aren’t always necessary
    Bails, the horizontal pegs that side astride the stumps, are seen in every game of cricket played.They’re not mandatory in all circumstances.
  • The oldest living test cricketer is 94
    The oldest living Test cricketer still around today is South Africa’s Ronald Draper,who’s almost 95 years old. He played his last test match in 1950.
  • The longest cricket match lasted....
    Cricket can be lengthy at the best of times. In 1939 a game between England and South Africa lasted 14 days.
